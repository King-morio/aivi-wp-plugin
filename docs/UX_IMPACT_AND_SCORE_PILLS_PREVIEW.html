AiVI UX Approval Preview

Impact Pills and Score Quality Treatments

These quick concepts keep AiVI supportive and premium while helping users understand what deserves action first. They also test a feasible ring-color upgrade for AEO and GEO: use a small set of score bands so a very low score no longer stays visually "healthy" just because the ring is short.

Impact labels The rail uses High impact, Recommended, and Polish so users can triage without seeing machine-like confidence scores.
Score pill The safest first rollout adds one quality pill to the global score card only. AEO and GEO stay numeric-first unless we later decide to add more interpretation there.
Ring colors Feasible and not over the top if kept banded: red for very weak, amber for fair, green for good, teal for excellent. Percentage-based bands keep AEO and GEO consistent despite different max values.
